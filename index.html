How have Food Imports to the U.S. changed over time

This project involves understanding how have Food Imports to the U.S. changed over the past 20 years. As Americans have become wealthier and more ethnically diverse, the American food basket currently reflects a growing share of tropical products, spices, and imported gourmet products. Seasonal and climatic factors drive U.S. imports of popular types of fruits and vegetables and tropical products, such as cocoa and coffee. Through this project, we aim to provide a better understanding of what the trends are and how they have changed over the past few years. Further, we aim to extract insights that can help answer the reasons for such changes and how have the various food groups been affected over the years.

Annual Food Imports by Geography

Globalization contributes greatly to feeding Americans. While the population has increased roughly 19%, the imports have grown nearly 300%. Trading relationships with our neighbors to the North and South have always been high due to proximity, but the reduced cost of shipping have contributed to increasing competitive advantages countries like China and the number of food imports have increased dramatically.

Insights


2008 Subprime Mortgage Crisis

During the Subprime Mortgage Crisis, imports dropped from an average 8% increase, to a decrease of 6%. A 11% drop in the trend.

Insights


Consumers Finding Alternative Goods

After the 2008 financial crisis, imports went down in all categories. However, wine imports, especially French wine imports were hit harder. Since the production of wine can take years, wine producers take a risk with their forecasting models. As you can see, French wine prices dropped considerably more than Italian wine, it appears that due to financial insecurity, consumers opted the less expensive Italian wines in favor of the French.

Insights


Mad Cows

In late 2003 and early 2004, Canadian officials had reported several cases of Bovine Spongiform Encephalopathy, also known as Mad Cow Disease. The USDA banned live bovine imports from Canada, the largest foreign supplier of beef to the U.S. causing a dramatic drop in live animal imports in to the U.S. until faith in the Canadian supply of beef was restored in 2008.

Insights


Turmoil in Middle East

For the past 2 decades the Middle East has been under turmoil. We expected that the imports from middle east would have seen a drastic impact. But our hypothesis has been proven wrong as along with other continents the Middle East has also seen significant increase in export of food to the USA.

Effect of Fair and Equitable Tobacco Reform Act 2004

In the year 2004 George Bush's govt removed the quota of production of tobacco on farmers and de-regulated the tobaco production. It was thought that this will make the US tobacco competitive to other country's tobacco prices and would result in less imports and more of job creation in USA. We looked at 2004 , 2005 and 2006 data for tobacco imports . We see that in year 2005 the imports went down but then again increased in 2006. Which implies that in tobacco its not only about price.
