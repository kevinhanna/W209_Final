How Food Imports to the U.S. Have Changed Over Time

This project involves understanding how food imports to the US have changed over the past 20 years. As Americans have become wealthier and more ethnically diverse, the American food basket currently reflects a growing share of tropical products, spices, and imported gourmet products. Seasonal and climatic factors drive U.S. imports of popular types of fruits and vegetables and tropical products, such as cocoa and coffee. Through this project, we aim to provide a better understanding of what the trends are and how they have changed over the past few years. Further, we aim to extract insights that can help answer the reasons for such changes and how have the various food groups been affected over the years.

Insights


2008 Subprime Mortgage Crisis

During the Subprime Mortgage Crisis, imports dropped from an average of 8% increase, to a decrease of 6%. A 11% drop from the previous year.

Insights


Mad Cows

In late 2003 and early 2004, Canadian officials had reported several cases of Bovine Spongiform Encephalopathy, also known as Mad Cow Disease. The USDA banned live bovine imports from Canada, the largest foreign supplier of beef to the U.S. causing a dramatic drop in live animal imports in to the U.S. until faith in the Canadian supply of beef was restored in 2008.
